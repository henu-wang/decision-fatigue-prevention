## What Is Decision Fatigue?

Decision fatigue is the phenomenon where the quality of decisions deteriorates after a long session of decision-making. It's not laziness — it's a measurable depletion of mental resources.

**Symptoms:**
- Avoiding decisions entirely (procrastination)
- Making impulsive choices (ego depletion)
- Choosing the default/status quo (path of least resistance)
- Irritability and mental fog
- Poor self-control (willpower depletion)

**The key insight:** Your brain has a finite daily budget for making decisions. Every choice — from what to wear to strategic business decisions — draws from the same pool.

## The Science

### The Israeli Parole Study

Researchers analyzed 1,112 judicial decisions over 10 months. Judges granted parole at different rates depending on the time of day:

| Time | Parole Grant Rate |
|------|:-:|
| After breakfast | ~65% |
| Before lunch | ~10% |
| After lunch | ~65% |
| End of day | ~10% |

Same judges, same types of cases. The only variable: decision fatigue. When depleted, judges defaulted to the safer option (deny parole).

### Baumeister's Ego Depletion Research

Roy Baumeister's experiments showed that:
- Making choices depletes the same resource as self-control
- After making many decisions, people are worse at: resisting temptation, persisting on hard tasks, doing math, and making good choices
- Glucose (energy) partially restores decision-making capacity

### The Average Person Makes 35,000 Decisions Per Day

Most are trivial (what to eat, what to wear, where to look), but they all draw from the same mental energy pool. The goal is to reduce trivial decisions so you have capacity for important ones.

## Strategies

### 1. Reduce Daily Decisions

**Principle:** Eliminate unnecessary choices from your day.

**Specific actions:**

| Area | Decision to Eliminate | How |
|------|---------------------|-----|
| **Clothing** | "What should I wear?" | Create a capsule wardrobe; wear uniforms |
| **Food** | "What should I eat?" | Meal plan on Sunday; repeat meals |
| **Morning** | "What should I do first?" | Fixed morning routine |
| **Email** | "Should I respond to this now?" | Check email at set times only |
| **Meetings** | "Should I attend this?" | Default no; require agenda to attend |
| **Shopping** | "Which product to buy?" | Buy the same brands/products repeatedly |

**Famous practitioners:**
- Steve Jobs: Same black turtleneck + jeans daily
- Mark Zuckerberg: Gray t-shirt daily
- Barack Obama: Only gray or blue suits ("I don't want to make decisions about what I'm eating or wearing, because I have too many other decisions to make")

### 2. Front-Load Important Decisions

**Principle:** Schedule your most important decisions for when your mental energy is highest (typically morning).

```
Morning (high energy)    → Strategic decisions, creative work, complex analysis
Midday (moderate)        → Routine meetings, email, administrative tasks
Afternoon (low energy)   → Simple tasks, follow-up, planning for tomorrow
Evening (depleted)       → Don't make important decisions; plan/prepare instead
```

**Template:**
```markdown
## Daily Decision Schedule

### High-energy window (___AM - ___AM):
- [ ] Most important decision/task: ____
- [ ] Deep work block: ____

### Moderate-energy window:
- [ ] Meetings and calls
- [ ] Routine decisions

### Low-energy window:
- [ ] Admin tasks
- [ ] Tomorrow's planning
- [ ] NO major decisions

### Off-limits after ___PM:
- Financial decisions
- Relationship conversations
- Career decisions
- Emails requiring careful response
```

### 3. Create Decision Rules

**Principle:** Pre-decide recurring choices with simple rules (if-then).

**Examples:**

| Category | Decision Rule |
|----------|-------------|
| **Invitations** | "If it's not a 'hell yes,' it's a no" |
| **Purchases** | "If it costs > $100, wait 48 hours" |
| **Meetings** | "If there's no agenda, I don't attend" |
| **Hiring** | "If any interviewer says no, it's a no" |
| **Investment** | "If it fails my checklist, I don't invest" |
| **Email** | "If it takes < 2 minutes, reply now; otherwise, batch it" |
| **Social media** | "No social media before noon" |
| **Diet** | "No dessert on weekdays" |
| **Exercise** | "Monday, Wednesday, Friday — non-negotiable" |

**The power:** Each rule eliminates thousands of in-the-moment decisions over a year. You can build and maintain a library of these rules using [KeepRule](https://keeprule.com), making them available whenever you need them.

### 4. Batch Similar Decisions

**Principle:** Group similar decisions together and handle them all at once.

| Decisions | Batch Schedule |
|-----------|---------------|
| Email responses | 2x/day (10am, 3pm) |
| Grocery shopping | Once per week (Sunday) |
| Meal planning | Once per week |
| Bill payments | 1st of each month (automate if possible) |
| Content creation | One day per week |
| Admin tasks | Friday afternoons |
| Strategic planning | Monthly half-day |

### 5. Use Defaults and Routines

**Principle:** Make the default option the right option so you don't have to decide.

**Morning routine (eliminate ~20 decisions):**
```
6:00 — Wake (alarm, no snooze debate)
6:05 — Water + vitamins (no deciding what to drink)
6:15 — Exercise (pre-set workout, no choosing)
7:00 — Shower + standard outfit
7:20 — Standard breakfast
7:40 — Start work with pre-determined #1 priority
```

**Financial defaults:**
- Auto-transfer savings on payday
- Auto-invest into index funds
- Auto-pay all bills
- Pre-set charitable donations

### 6. Simplify Your Environment

**Principle:** Design your physical and digital environment to reduce choices.

| Area | Simplification |
|------|---------------|
| **Closet** | Fewer clothes, coordinating colors |
| **Kitchen** | Fewer ingredients, standard recipes |
| **Digital** | Fewer apps, notification off, clean desktop |
| **Workspace** | Clear desk, single-task setup |
| **Phone** | Delete social media apps; use browser only |
| **Subscriptions** | Cancel anything you don't use monthly |

> "For every possession you own, you must make decisions about it — maintain it, store it, insure it, clean it, worry about it." — Simplifying your environment simplifies your decision load.

### 7. Protect Recovery Time

**Principle:** Decision-making capacity regenerates with rest, food, and sleep.

**Recovery methods:**
| Method | Effect | Time |
|--------|--------|------|
| **Sleep** | Full restoration | 7-9 hours |
| **Naps** | Partial restoration | 20-30 minutes |
| **Meals** | Glucose restores some capacity | Post-meal boost |
| **Nature walks** | Attention restoration theory | 20+ minutes |
| **Meditation** | Reduces cognitive load | 10+ minutes |
| **Exercise** | Improves cognitive function | 30+ minutes |

**The depletion warning signs:**
- You're procrastinating on simple decisions
- You're irritable over minor things
- You're craving junk food or sugar
- You're defaulting to "whatever" or "I don't care"
- You've been making decisions non-stop for 2+ hours

**When you notice these:** Stop deciding. Take a break, eat, or defer to tomorrow.

---

### 8. Delegate and Automate

**Principle:** Every decision you delegate or automate is one fewer decision depleting your capacity.

**Delegation framework:**
```
For each recurring decision, ask:
1. Does this require MY judgment specifically? → If no, delegate
2. Can this be automated with a rule? → If yes, automate
3. Can this be eliminated entirely? → If yes, eliminate
4. If none of the above → Keep, but schedule for peak energy
```

**Automation examples:**
- Bill payments → Auto-pay
- Investment contributions → Auto-invest
- Email sorting → Filters and rules
- Scheduling → Calendar tool with booking link
- Grocery shopping → Recurring delivery order
